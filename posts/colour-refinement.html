Colour Refinement and the Weisfeiler–Leman Algorithm

Colour refinement (also known as naive vertex refinement) is a simple and extremely efficient algorithm used to iteratively distinguish vertices in a graph.

Algorithm

Initialization: all vertices receive the same colour.

Refinement step: two vertices get different colours if they have different numbers of neighbours of some colour.

The process repeats until the colouring becomes stable.

As an Isomorphism Test

Apply the algorithm to the disjoint union of two graphs. If the final colour distribution differs, the graphs are not isomorphic.

  • Distinguishes all trees
  • Fails on some regular graphs

Connection to WL

This is exactly the 1-dimensional Weisfeiler–Leman algorithm (1-WL).
